## Design Context

### Users
DevOps and platform engineers managing Kubernetes-native application delivery. They're technical, time-pressed, and need clarity over flash. They use Paprika to replace ArgoCD + Argo Rollouts + Argo Workflows with one operator. The dashboard is their daily control panel.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp, confident, warm.** Approachable but technically serious. Like a well-designed tool — it doesn't need to shout. The paprika orange is warm and memorable but used sparingly.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark mode only (developer tool, ops context)
- Warm-tinted neutrals (never pure gray/black)
- Orange primary as sharp accent used at 10% volume — most of the UI is surface tones
- No glassmorphism, no purple/cyan gradients, no glowing accents
- Clean, precise, industrial with warmth in the details
- Visual signature: the pipeline flow visualization as hero graphic
- Typography: clean but characterful (instrument sans + jetbrains mono)

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** — every visual element earns its place. The UI gets out of the way.
2. **Warmth in the details** — subtle warm tints in neutrals, rich orange accents, space to breathe.
3. **Surface hierarchy** — depth comes from surface lightness, not shadows or blur.
4. **Sparse accent** — orange is powerful because it's rare. Most of the UI is in-surface tones.
5. **One signature moment** — the pipeline flow viz is the hero. Everything else supports it.
